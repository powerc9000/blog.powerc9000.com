It makes sense, when you look at the math, why everything regardless of mass falls at the same rate.

The equation for the force of gravity looks like this \[F = \frac{Gravitational\ Constant*Our\ Mass*Earth's\ Mass}{distance^2}\]

The equation for acceleration looks like this: \[a = \frac {f}{m}\]

If we want to find acceleration due to gravitational force: \[\frac {\frac{Gravitational\ Constant*Our\ Mass*Earth's\ Mass}{distance^2}}{Our\ Mass}\] simplifies to \[\require{cancel}\] \[\frac{Gravitational\ Constant* \cancel{Our\ Mass}*Earth's\ Mass}{distance^2* \cancel{Our\ Mass}}\] \[\frac{Gravitational\ Constant*Earth's\ Mass}{distance^2}\]

So the whole point of this is that our mass doesn’t matter when falling to earth that’s why all things fall at the same rate. This is actually how we get \(\frac{9.8m}{s^2}\) as the rate all things fall.

\[\frac {(6.67384*10^{-11} m^3\ kg^{-1}\ s^{-2}) * (5.972*10^{24} kg)}{(6,371,000 m)^2}\] \[\frac {3.985617248*10^{14} m^3\ s^{-2}}{(4.0589641*10^{13} m^2)} \approx \frac{9.81929662m}{s^2}\]
